# Second Kings

## Introduction


### Author
The same author of 1st Kings continues with 2nd Kings, namely Jeremiah. It is remarkable that 2 Kings 24:18-25:30 is almost the same as Jeremiah 52.


### Date
The last 2 chapters were written around 550 B.C. after the release of king Jehoiachin in 560 B.C. while most of 1 and 2 Kings were written most likely prior to 586 B.C.


### Setting
The Jewish United Kingdom existed for 112 years (1043-931 B.C.), then the divided kingdom (Northern Kingdom of Israel and the Southern kingdom of Judah) pushed for another 209 years (931-722 B.C.), with the Northern Kingdom being deported into captivity in 722 B.C. by Assyria.
The Southern kingdom continued for another 136 years (722-586 B.C.) and then carried to captivity by Babylon. This gives a total of 457 years of the kingdom existence up to captivity period.
- Northern Israel had 19 consecutive evil kings hence it had 9 dynasties from different tribes and was the first to go to captivity.
- The Southern kingdom of Judah on the other hand had 20 kings and 8 of them made some positive spiritual reforms, and as God had promised, all were from the same lineage of David.
- Due to some periods of spiritual revival in Judah, the kingdom pushed some 136 years more after Northern Israel had been scattered into captivity.
God sent prophets to both kingdoms in a bid to turn them from their ways of perdition.
Northern Israel had Elijah, Elisha, Amos and Hosea, while Judah had Obadiah, Joel, Isaiah, Micah, Nahum, Zephaniah, Jeremiah and Habakkuk
- Both kingdoms eventually went into captivity due to their apostasy.


### Message
- The Devil was trying to mar the whole Jewish race in a bid to thwart the plan of Salvation since Jesus was supposed to be born from it.
- Nevertheless the Lord kept a remnant down through the centuries till Christ came down on earth and was born from the tribe of Judah, David's lineage.
Elijah was a type John the Baptist **(Matthew 11:14; 17:10-12; Luke 1:17),** he (Elijah) lived away from people, emphasized law, judgement repentance.
- On the other hand Elisha was a type of Christ, living among the people and stressing grace, life and hope.
- NB: A true Christian to be wholesome, should embrace all these tenets from both Elijah and Elisha i.e. (Law, judgement, repentance, grace, life and hope).


### Prayer
As we delve into the second book of kings, continue to speak to us in a way we will understand you, Amen.

## 2 Kings 1 — The death of Ahaziah king of Israel

*The death of Ahaziah king of Israel*


### Key Lessons


#### 1. Is there no God in Israel
Having ruled for only 2 years, Ahaziah the evil son of Ahab fell from the window and injured himself.
He sent messengers to inquire of his health from Baal Zebub the god of Ekron
- Elijah intercepted them and sent them back to ask the king whether there was no God in Israel that he wanted to inquire from the god of Ekron.
- Ekron was some 88.5km Southwest of Samaria as compared to Jerusalem which was 64.4km to the South.
- Hence Ahaziah decides to send his messengers far to a foreign land of the Philistines than just go the temple in Jerusalem which was nearer to inquire from the Lord.
- Even nearer still was Elijah who was from the same Northern Kingdom.
- Sin blinds us to the help that is close to us and within our reach, and sends us to faraway and wrong places looking for solutions that would end up destroying us.
- How often do we sneak out of church to go and look for help that has already been supplied therein, you talk of the ideal diet, dress code, music, education, recreation etc.


#### 2. The destruction of the messengers
Having received the rebuke by his messengers from the man of God, Ahaziah sends a captain with his 50 soldiers to capture Elijah but the prophet calls down fire and it consumes them. A second squad of 50 is sent and likewise it perishes by fire. Only the captain of the 3rd batch goes and pleads with Elijah to spare his life and his soldiers.
Only then is Elijah instructed by God to go with him to Ahaziah and to repeat the impending demise of the king for his bid to consult foreign gods.
- Many a people are prepared find fault or worse still eliminate God's messengers who reprove their sins than simply repenting.


#### 3. The death of Ahaziah
Even after Elijah confirms the king's sin and punishment, Ahaziah does not repent of his guilt and gets to die in his sins.
- Some of us will harden our hearts till will go down to our graves having spurned all the efforts to bring us back to the path of salvation.
- _"Through spiritualism many of the sick, the bereaved, the curious, are communicating with evil spirits. All who venture to do this are on dangerous ground. The word of truth declares how God regards them. In ancient times He pronounced a stern judgment on a king who had sent for counsel to a heathen oracle;... Now therefore thus saith the Lord, Thou shalt not come down from that bed on which thou art gone up, but shalt surely die. _ *(Ellen G. White: Acts of the Apostles 290)*


### Prayer
To our Father in heaven, may we not forsake you in preference for other gods, Amen.


## 2 Kings 2 — Elijah is taken to heaven

*Elijah is taken to heaven*


### Key Lessons

- 1 . Commitment Pays*
Elijah repeatedly asked Elisha to remain behind as he proceeded but Elisha persistently stuck to him.
- By so doing Elisha showed commitment to the call he had received to replace Elijah.
We need to be true to our calling and to duty.
** "In his early labor of guiding the plow, Elisha had learned not to fail or to become discouraged; and now that he had set his hand to the plow in another line of duty, he would not be diverted from his purpose. As often as the invitation to turn back was given, his answer was, As the Lord liveth, and as thy soul liveth, I will not leave thee.*

- 2 . Requesting for a double portion*
Having shown his diligence and commitment to the work, Elijah asks Elisha to request what he would need, and he pleaded for a double portion of Elijah's spirit.
- Knowing the kind of calling and responsibility before him, Elisha needed God's guidance in full measure.
- To all those who humbly and earnestly ask God, He will not withhold his blessing and gift of the Holy Spirit on them.

- 3 . The importance of mentorship.*
God instructed Elijah to mentor Elisha, and after Elijah was gone, the work did not halt but marched on.
- We are to coach those we lead to take over from us. Let not the work perish with a few people but let the responsibilities be shared with others all around.

- 4 . Elijah is translated to heaven*
Elijah was separated from Elisha by a chariot and horses of fire and taken to heaven by a whirlwind.
- Elijah's experience instills us with hope of the Second Coming of Christ when all the saints will beat the force of gravity and meet the Lord in the air.
** "Elijah was a type of the saints who will be living on the earth at the time of the second advent of Christ and who will be changed, in a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump, without tasting of death."*

- 5 . Danger of mocking God's servants*
The unscrupulous children mocked Elisha and called him names and taunting him to also go up to heaven and follow Elijah.
- His curse led to their demise with bears.
- We are to teach our children to be respectful to Elders and to leaders lest a curse rests upon them.
- Adults are not exempt from the the deference due to leaders


### Prayer
In the name of the Father, Son and Holy Spirit, may Elijah's experience be ours when you come to take us home. Amen.


## 2 Kings 3 — Moab rebels against Israel and is defeated by a joint army.

*Moab rebels against Israel and is defeated by a joint army.*


### Key Lessons


#### 1. When Family members quarrel
When Moab rebelled against Israel, Jehoram the king requested Jehoshaphat's hand and that of the king of Edom to fight the Moabites.
- This could be called a family dispute in that Moab was a descendant of Lot (Gen 19:37), a son born out of an incestuous act by Lot's first born daughter and her father after getting him drunk.
- Edom was a nation descendant from Esau *(Genesis 36:1)*
- Israel and Judah were children of Jacob, hence all these 4 nations engaged in war were relatives.
- Sin came to separate and cause divisions even among family members, not to mention church and work mates
- If only all these 4 nations and all of us could heed Abraham's call to Lot, "let there be no strife between you and me...for we are brethren."


#### 2. Avoid bad company
It seems with all the goodness of Jehoshaphat, he lacked a backbone of saying no to bad relationships.
He allowed his son to marry Ahab's daughter, once he had accompanied Ahab in a war that God had specifically opposed through prophet Micaiah and he nonetheless went ahead, and it almost cost his life.
Here again, Jehoram, Ahab's son and successor requests Jehoshaphat for help in a war and he agrees to go.
- May we learn to say no to bad company, and not try to please men at the expense of God.
- Here again God through Elisha scolded and showed no regard for the king of the North for his evil
- Bad company corrupts good morals *(1 Corinthians 15:33).*


#### 3. God working in spite of us
God worked out a miracle and the confederate kings defeated the Moabites.
- Sometimes God works through us and gives us success not because of our goodness but because of His name's sake.
- If we could only grab such opportunities to make right with God and men.


### Prayer
Why should we fight for we are brethren, grant it to us Lord that we live peacebly. Amen.

*The Miracle of the widow's oil, the Shunammite's son, the pot of stew and the feeding of the one hundred.*

#### 1. Shun debt like leprosy
One of the sons of the prophets died and left debt for his family
As much as possible, we must not leave behind troubles that will affect our families.
- We need to put our houses in order while we are still alive.
- As much as possible we are to avoid debt.
- God used what the woman had, a little oil, to get her out of the debt and for her sustenance


#### 2. Hospitality repaid
The Shunammite woman and her husband were very rich and hospitable.
They welcomed the man of God and provided for his needs whenever he passed by
- God repaid their generosity by giving them a child through Elisha's promise.
- When the boy died, through the service of Elisha God raised him up
- We are to remember to be hospitable always.


#### 3. Miracles on food
At Gilgal, Elisha healed the pot of stew and turned the poisonous dish to be edible.
Again, Elisha fed one hundred people from some little food.
- God provides for our needs. All we need to do is ask and believe.
- _"What condescension it was on the part of Christ, through His messenger, to work this miracle to satisfy hunger! Again and again since that time, though not always in so marked and perceptible a manner, has the Lord Jesus worked to supply human need. If we had clearer spiritual discernment we would recognize more readily than we do God s compassionate dealing with the children of men."_ *(Ellen G. White: Prophets and Kings 241)*


#### 4. Theological Nugget
A deeper meaning lay under all these miracles.
- In a land where many were following Baal, such miracles were proving where real power lay.
- Baal was believed to be a god of fertility, yet the giving of a child to the childless couple showed God as the true solution
- The famine that plagued the land proved Baal useless.

## 2 Kings 5 — Naaman is healed of leprosy but Gehazi is stricken of them.

*Naaman is healed of leprosy but Gehazi is stricken of them.*


### Key Lessons
- 1 . Faith knows no bounds*
A young girl's faith in God replicated in the Syrian commander and king and Naaman went to seek for help in Israel.
The young girl did not see in her master's sickness a way of getting even.
In fact, it had been her master who had led the Syrian conquest that had brought her into slavery, but that did not deter her from showing sympathy and pity to her captor.
- What a lesson to us all on forgiveness and loving those who hate and trouble us.
** "The conduct of the captive maid, the way that she bore herself in that heathen home, is a strong witness to the power of early home training. There is no higher trust than that committed to fathers and mothers in the care and training of their children. Parents have to do with the very foundations of habit and character. By their example and teaching the future of their children is largely decided."*

- 2 . Be mindful of your worldview*
The Israelite king interpreted Naaman's visit and the king's request to make his commander whole using political lenses.
By so doing he perceived that this was a trap meant to ignite a war between Israel and Syria.
- Nevertheless, Elisha requested the king to him, since this was a great opportunity to introduce the true God of heaven to the unbelieving echelons of Syria.
- The way we view matters can hinder progress if we do not look at them in a wholesome manner.

- 3 . The need of faith and humility*
As a great man, Naaman expected to be treated with the deference due to him.
What great disappointment was his when Elisha only sent out his servant instructing him to go dip himself 7 times in the Jordan river
- His faith and ego were severely tested, and he angrily rehearsed how he had thought he would be treated.
- However God does not work in our terms but we in His.
- With the counsel from his own servants, Naaman reluctantly obeyed and was made whole.
- We are to follow God's biddings for they are his enabling acts.

- 4 . Naaman's conversion*
Naaman returned to Elisha announcing his conversion to the Israelite God, and renounced the worship of Syrian false gods.
He requested to carry 2 mule loads of soil from Israel most likely because
a. Even if he had believed in the true God, as a new convert he still had the ancient belief that God would be restricted to His land and people, hence he wanted to carry that soil and be able to worship that God in his land. (Light is progressive and God pardons us in our times of ignorance, for He is a universal God hence even the successes in war made by Naaman had been credited to God (vs 1) who owns the whole everything).
b. it could also have been an affirmation of faith by Naaman as he now viewed himself as an Israelite and hence claiming his inheritance figuratively by taking with him part of the land, though this might less likely could have been the reason.
Naaman excused himself whenever he would escort the king into the temples of the Syrian gods, and explained that he would not be worshipping those gods.
- He called the name of his former god Rimmon (vs 18). The real name was Ramanu, meaning the thunderer, which was a reference to Baal the storm god.
- Hence changing the vowels to Rimmon alters the meaning to pomegranate, that is, the thunderer is reduced to a pomegranate.
- It could be assumed that by this Naaman was renouncing his former faith in false gods and religion.

- 5 . The penalty of greed and falsehood*
Gehazi secretly pursued Naaman and requested the gifts that had been refused by Elisha, pretending as if he had been sent by the man of God.
Having achieved his goal,upon his return he received a curse from Elisha and received Naaman leprosy.
- What an irony, a non believer came and got healed and was converted to the God of Israel, yet a servant of Elisha the man of God, coverted and lied (fell for the false Syrian worship) and received the leprosy.
- The curse that physically plagued Gehazi, symbolically plagued the nation of Israel too. In that Naaman the Syrian renounced his worship of Baal, yet Israel attached herself to Baalism and thus received a curse of 'leprosy" on her, ie. (Israel's judgement and destruction).


### Prayer
We would be healed of our leprosy of sin and we shall be clean from all our filth, dear Lord, amen.

## 2 Kings 12 — Joash reigns in Jerusalem

*Joash reigns in Jerusalem*


### Key Lessons


#### 1. Joash reigns in Jerusalem
Joash or Jehoash began ruling at 7 years of age at the tutelage of the priest Jehoiada
- Through his influence, the king did that which was right before the Lord.
- May we be Jehoiada(s) today and influence others for good.


#### 2. Financial Dishonesty
The duty to collect finances and repair the temple was given to priests during the early years of the king.
Nevertheless, the work lay idle till the 23rd year of king Joash.
Upon inquiry, the king could have picked that the priests were misappropriating the funds to their own ends which was meant for the temple repairs for all those years.
- He had to stop them from receiving that money and made his scribe and High Priest responsible for its collection and disbursement.
- Thus the work progressed well.
- What a dark chapter this is, when the custodians of the Lord's treasuries are themselves found wanting, God forbid!


#### 3. Joash's fall
The king walked perfectly before God all the days of Jehoiada the priest and was influenced to revert back to idolatry by the leaders of Judah **(2 Chronicles 24:18)**
- Having begun well, the king nonetheless did not remove the high places, and this became a snare which later blossomed into fully fledged idolatry with people burning incense and worshipping there.
- Some people are only as good as there is a good person besides them, they do not have the backbone to stand up for God on their own as did the king.


#### 4. Joash Punished
Having backslidden, God allowed Hazael to come and conquer them **(2 Chronicles 24:15-25).**
Zechariah the son of Jehoiada protested against the backsliding and at the king's command they stoned him to death **(2 Chronicles 24:21)**
- What great treachery and ungratefulness! Killing the son of the man who had stood by the king all his years?
- Actually Joash owed his life to that faithful priest who had arranged the tight concealment and protection of the young king from Athaliah his murderous grandmother and arranged his crowing and the killing of the impostor Athaliah.
- And is this the way Joash chose to repay the good done to him?, That is top notch betrayal!
- How many of us have betrayed those who stood by us, put their lives on the line for our sake? God have mercy!


#### 5. Joash's Death
Hazael defeated and wounded Joash **(2 Chronicles 24: 24, 25).**
- Just as Asa had set a bad precedence, of taking the palace and temple wealth to appease a Syrian king *(1 Kings 15:14),* thus did Joash to Hazael the Syrian king.
- Two conspirators (the king's servants), both descendants of Lot *(Genesis 19:32-38),* one a son of an Ammonitess and the other a son of a Moabitess *(2 Chronicles 24: 26),* went and killed the king as he was nursing his wounds
- The names of the two were Zabad *(2 Chronicles 24:26)* or Jozachar meaning "Jehovah had remembered" and Jehozabad which means "Jehovah has given."
- It's noteworthy to recognize the match between the conspirators' names and Zechariah's last words as he was stoned, _"The Lord look upon it and require it"_ *(2 Chronicles 24:22),* that is "Let the Lord see and give vengeance"
- In as much as the king failed to remember the kindness of Jehoiada the priest to him, nonetheless the Lord remembered and gave vengeance. *(SDA Bible Commentary, 2 Kings 13:1)*
- Our sins will always find us out.
